
EDA 

EDA stands for exploratory Data analysis - its the step 
where you explore the data to :


Understand it 
Discover patterns
Spot anomalies
Generate insight 
And decide What to do next 



think of EDA as detective works- you're looking at your
data with curiosity before building any models 
or doing fancy transformations 



EDA steps

1. viewing the Data 
    head(), tail(), shape, info()
    what column do i have? what types of data?

2. Summary Statistics
    mean, median, mode, std, min, max, quartiles
    help Understand spread and central tendency

3. value count
    how many unique value in a column?
    great for categorical columns

4. Missing value analysis
    where are the gaps? what percent of the data is Missing?

5.Visualizations
    Histograms > distribution of value
    boxplots > outliers and spread
    bar plots > comparisons of categories
    correlation heatmap > linear relationship between numerical features
    Scatter plots > bivariate relationship

6. Target variable exploration
    How does your output (like "charges" in your dataset) relate to other variables?



Importance Of EDA 

1. you cant clean or preprocess what you dont Understand.
2.it helps you identify mistakes , biases , or limitations in the data .
3. it guides the direction of your data cleaning and features engineering.
4. it gives your audience or stakeholder a "Story" or overview of what the data is telling you.
